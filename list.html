8 Modern Musicals that Retell or Reimagine Greek Mythology

There has been a recent increase in the number of musicals being produced that either retell or reimagine well-known Greek myths. Although there have been mythological musicals since the first few decades of the genre's establishment in American culture, the uptick starting in 2019 with Hadestown is what I am focusing my master's project on. For my own reference, I have compiled this list of retellings and reimaginings.

The Golden Apple (1954)

By Jerome Moross and John Treville Latouche

The Golden Apple opened Off-Broadway on March 11, 1954. It is a turn-of-the-century retelling of The Iliad that takes place in Washington State in the town of Angel's Roost, near Mt. Olumpus. Menelaus's wife Helen runs off with a traveling salesman named Paris, who is visitin gthe town to judge an apple pie baking contest. Ulysses, who has just come back from military service, leaves his wife Penelope on a ten-year expedition to bring Helen back.

The Frogs (1974)

By Stephen Sondheim and Burt Shevelove

The Frogs is adapted from the ancient comedic play of the same name by Aristophanes. In the musical, the god Dionysos travels to Hades to bring George Bernard Shaw back from the dead. However, Dionysos ends up returning with Shakespeare instead, after he proves himself the better playwright. The god hopes Shakespeare's return will revitalize art and save civilization.

Paris: The Musical (1990)

By Jon English and David Mackay

Paris, like The Golden Apple, is an Iliad retelling. This time, though, the story is told from the Trojan hero and wife-stealer Paris's perspective through rock music. The musical is about Paris and Helen's love story, although other figures from the epic, like Menelaus, Achilles and Patroclus, do feature. Paris juggles themes of passion vs. order, law vs. chaos, and head vs. heart.

Xanadu on Broadway (2007)

By Douglas Carter Beane, Jeff Lynne and John Farrar

Xanadu is a musical comedy based on the 1980 film of the same name. In that same year, artist Sonny Malone is inspired by six of the ancient Greek Muses to create a roller disco. Over the course of the show, Sonny and one of the muses fall in love, but is that love real or just a fabrication of Eros's arrows?

Lysistrata Jones (2011)

By Douglas Carter Beane and Lewis Flinn

Lysistrata Jones is a modern, high school retelling of Aristophanes' comedy Lysistrata. In the musical, the men's basketball team at Athens University hasn't won a game in the last 30 years. Cheerleader Lysistrata Jones convinces the other girls at school to stop sleeping with members of the team until they finally win a game.

The Lightning Thief: The Percy Jackson Musical (2017)

By Rob Rokicki and Joe Tracz

The Lightning Thief is an adaptation of Rick Riordan's 2005 novel of the same name. It follows Percy Jackson, a young demigod son of Poseidon, as he learns about his godly roots and goes on a quest to save his mother and return Zeus's masterbolt to Olympus, where it had been stolen. He does so with the help of Grover, a satyr and his best friend, and Annabeth, a daughter of Athena who isn't sure what to make of this supposed "hero" of prophecy.

Hadestown (2019)

By By Anaïs Mitchell

Hadestown is a retelling of the Orpheus and Eurydice myth, set in a dystopian world where the seasons have been thrown out of sync by Hades and Persephone's failing relationship. The musical addresses how love can bloom and survive in late stage capitalism, where hunger drives people to extremes. When Eurydice follows Hades to his industrial city to escape the bitter cold, Orpheus must try to use his power of songwriting to save her and melt the heart of the King of the Underworld.

Stray Gods: The Roleplaying Musical (2023)

By Summerfall Studios

Stray Gods is a video game that allows players to choose their own musical adventure. They assume the role of Grace, a college dropout who has been accused of murdering the last muse, Calliope. Grace has to prove her innocence to Athena, Apollo, Persephone and Aphrodite, all within seven days. Having inherited Calliope's musical gift after her death, Grace uses song to try to learn the truth behind the muse's death.
