Adapting Antiquity Project Overview

For my master's project, I will be developing a long-form piece of narrative journalism that explores the recent trend of musical theater adaptations of Greek mythology. This project will seek to learn what draws composers to these myths, how they alter them to fit modern storytelling and themes, what the compositional process is like, and how cast members and tech crew, etc. contribute to the adaptation. Musicals that I will be looking at include:

  • The Frogs (1974)
  • Paris: The Musical (1990)
  • The Lightning Thief (2017)
  • Hadestown (2019)
  • Aristos (2023)
  • Stray Gods (2023)
  • EPIC: The Musical (2024)
  • Stories from Styx (2025)
  • Fatebound (in development)
  • Zodiac: The Musical (in development)

By looking at these specific musicals, I will also explore subtrends in these adaptations, including the focus on the Homeric epics and Underworld mythology, urban fantasy reimaginings and the recent influx of musicals being developed over social media platforms.

8 Modern Musicals that Retell or Reimagine Greek Mythology

There has been a recent increase in the number of musicals being produced that either retell or reimagine well-known Greek myths. Although there have been mythological musicals since the first few decades of the genre's establishment in American culture, the uptick starting in 2019 with Hadestown is what I am focusing my master's project on. For my own reference, I have compiled this list of retellings and reimaginings.

The Golden Apple (1954)

By Jerome Moross and John Treville Latouche

The Golden Apple is a modern retelling of Helen of Troy leaving her husband Menelaus for Paris. Ulysses helps Menelaus try to get her back.

The Frogs (1974)

By Stephen Sondheim and Burt Shevelove

The Frogs retells Aristophanes's play by the same name. In it, the god Dionysos travels to Hades to bring George Bernard Shaw back from the dead.

Paris: The Musical (1990)

By Jon English and David Mackay

Paris is a rock opera retelling of the Iliad from Paris's perspective. It features other heroes like Achilles, Patroclus, Menelaus and Agamemnon.

Xanadu on Broadway (2007)

By Douglas Carter Beane, Jeff Lynne and John Farrar

Xanadu is a musical comedy based on the 1980 film of the same name. Artist Sonny Malone is inspired by six of the Muses to create a roller disco.

Lysistrata Jones (2011)

By Douglas Carter Beane and Lewis Flinn

Based on Aristophanes' comedy Lysistrata, the girls of Athens University stop sleeping with the school's basketball team until they can win a game..

The Lightning Thief (2017)

By Rob Rokicki and Joe Tracz

An adaptation of Rick Riordan's 2005 novel of the same name. Percy Jackson, son of Poseidon, has to return Zeus's masterbolt and save his mom.

Hadestown (2019)

By By Anaïs Mitchell

Hadestown is a retelling of the Orpheus and Eurydice myth, set in a dystopian world where the seasons have been thrown out of sync by Hades and Persephone's failing relationship.

Stray Gods: The Roleplaying Musical (2023)

By Summerfall Studios

Stray Gods is a video game that allows players to choose their own musical adventure. They assume the role of Grace, a college dropout who has been accused of murdering the last muse, Calliope.
